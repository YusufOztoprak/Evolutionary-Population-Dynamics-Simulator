What Does Evolution Mean Here?

In this simulator, evolution is modeled as a gradual change in the distribution of genetic traits within a population.

Individuals with traits closer to the environmental optimum are more likely to survive and reproduce. Over generations, the population "shifts" towards this optimal state, demonstrating adaptation.

Fitness: Not Strength, But Fit

Fitness represents how well an individual is adapted to its environment. It is not about physical strength, but about how close an individual's genetic trait is to the environment's requirement.

Higher fitness increases the probability of survival and reproduction. However, fitness is relative; as the environment changes (or if the optimum shifts), what was once "fit" may no longer be sufficient.

Mutation: The Engine of Diversity

Mutation introduces random variation into genetic traits. Without mutation, evolution would stagnate because the population would simply consist of clones of the initial ancestors.

Small mutations allow the population to "explore" nearby trait values, gradually climbing the hill of fitness. Large mutations are riskier but can sometimes lead to breakthroughs.

Population Dynamics

The environment has limited resources (Carrying Capacity). As population size increases, competition intensifies.

This creates a balance between growth (reproduction) and death (competition), preventing unlimited population expansion. This is why you see the population curve flatten out over time.
